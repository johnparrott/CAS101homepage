CAS 101 presents an introduction to major concepts, theories, and methods in the study of physical, cognitive, social, and emotional development from conception through adolescence. Development and practical applications will be considered within different biological, familial, and cultural contexts. The course is designed to provide a basic foundation of important concepts and theories to be developed further in subsequent CAS courses.

Required Text

Child Growth and Development

Jennifer Paris, Antoinette Ricardo, Dawn Rymond, and Alexa Johnson (2019)

Course Objective

  • Students will gain an understanding of the major concepts, theories, and methods in the study of physical, cognitive, social, and emotional development from conception through adolescence.
  • Students will examine major concepts, theories, and methods related to child development within different biological, familial, racial, cultural, and political contexts.
  • Students will apply knowledge about child development to practical applications in multiple settings.

Student Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Describe important developmental outcomes related to physical, cognitive, social, and emotional development from conception through adolescence.
  • Describe major theories in the field of child development.
  • Recognize different research methodologies used to study child development.
  • Discuss socioeconomic, cultural, and racial influences on development within a historical and political context.
  • Explain the significance of early experiences to later outcomes.
  • Discuss current trends in the field of child development, such as media use and education.
